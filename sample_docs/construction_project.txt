CONSTRUCTION PROJECT SAFETY BRIEF
Project: Riverside Mixed-Use Development — Tower A
Location: Downtown, River District
Height: 28 floors | 110 meters
Construction Type: Cast-in-place concrete + structural steel

PROJECT SCOPE
==============
Construction of a 28-story mixed-use tower comprising:
- Floors B2–B1: Underground parking (240 spaces)
- Floors 1–4: Retail and commercial space
- Floors 5–28: Residential apartments (186 units)
- Rooftop: Mechanical penthouse + green roof terrace

CONSTRUCTION PHASES
====================
Phase 1 (Months 1–6): Site preparation, excavation, deep foundations (drilled shafts)
Phase 2 (Months 7–14): Below-grade concrete structure, waterproofing
Phase 3 (Months 15–26): Above-grade structure (floors 1–28), concurrent MEP rough-in
Phase 4 (Months 27–36): Facade, fit-out, commissioning

WORKFORCE & SITE CONDITIONS
=============================
- Peak workforce: ~350 workers on site simultaneously
- Multiple subcontractors: Concrete, steel, MEP, facade, finishing
- Adjacent structures: Existing 6-story office building (5m clearance)
- Underground utilities: High-pressure gas main 3m from excavation zone
- Public pedestrian zone: Active sidewalk on two sides
- River proximity: Site boundary 15m from riverbank

MAJOR ACTIVITIES & EQUIPMENT
==============================
- Deep excavation: 12m below grade, dewatering required
- Tower cranes: 3x luffing-jib cranes (max hook height 140m)
- Concrete pours: High-rise pump (up to 110m vertical)
- Structural steel erection: Floors 1–4 and mechanical levels
- Facade installation: Unitized curtain wall from swing stage/mast climbers
- Below-grade waterproofing: Torch-applied membrane (open flame)

IDENTIFIED SITE-SPECIFIC RISKS (PRELIMINARY)
==============================================
1. Fall hazards: Open floor edges, stairwell openings, leading edge work
2. Crane operations: Overlapping crane zones, nearby structures, wind exposure
3. Excavation: Deep excavation next to gas main and adjacent building
4. Concrete: High-rise pump failures, form failure, workers above active pours
5. Steel erection: Connections above, falling objects, working at height
6. Confined spaces: Underground parking, utility vaults, tank installation
7. Dewatering: Groundwater management, pump failure, adjacent foundation impacts
8. Fire: Torch-applied waterproofing, temporary heating, combustible materials
9. Public safety: Tower crane radius over pedestrian areas
10. Night work: Planned for concrete pours (noise, visibility, fatigue)

EXISTING SAFETY PROGRAM ELEMENTS
===================================
- Site safety manager: 1 FTE (CHST certified)
- Subcontractor safety orientation: Required, 4-hour program
- Morning toolbox talks: Daily
- PPE: Hard hat, high-vis, safety boots mandatory
- Fall protection: 100% tie-off policy above 6 feet
- Crane lift plan: Required for all critical lifts

GAPS IDENTIFIED (SELF-ASSESSMENT)
===================================
1. Rescue plan for fall arrest activations not documented
2. Hot work permit system not yet established
3. No confined space program — needed for dewatering sumps
4. Crane overlap zone management plan not finalized
5. Emergency evacuation plan for high-rise in construction not done
6. Utility strike prevention plan pending survey completion
7. Public protection plan not approved by city yet
